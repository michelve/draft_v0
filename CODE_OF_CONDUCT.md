# Code of Conduct

**Short version: be kind, assume good intent, and keep the conversation focused on the work.**

## The basics

Draft v0 is a small, open project. Everyone who shows up here - opening issues, leaving comments, submitting PRs, joining discussions - deserves to be treated with respect.

That means:

- Be welcoming and patient, especially with people who are new or stuck
- Disagree with ideas, not people - technical debates are healthy, personal attacks are not
- Give feedback that you'd be comfortable receiving yourself
- Own your mistakes; apologize when you get something wrong and move on
- Remember there's a human on the other side of every comment

Things that are not okay here:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Insulting, derogatory, or dismissive comments
- Sharing someone's private information without their consent
- Sexualized language or imagery
- Continuing to engage with someone after they've asked you to stop

This applies to GitHub Issues, PRs, Discussions, and any other space connected to this project.

## If something goes wrong

If you see behavior that violates this, or if something happens to you:

- Open a [GitHub Issue](https://github.com/michelve/draft_v0/issues) for things that can be discussed publicly
- Contact the maintainer privately through GitHub for anything sensitive

All reports are taken seriously. If the concern is valid, action will be taken - ranging from a private message all the way to a permanent ban depending on severity.

Your privacy as a reporter will be respected.

## Consequences

The maintainer will use judgment based on impact and context. In rough order of severity:

1. **Correction** - a private note explaining what was wrong and why
2. **Warning** - a formal warning with expectations for future behavior
3. **Temporary ban** - time-limited removal from participation
4. **Permanent ban** - for repeated or severe violations
